  • 2.John Milton's Comus
  • Characters:-
  • Two younger Brothers: Two unnamed brothers who represent reason and steadfastness.
  • The Lady: The central character, also referred to as the Lady Attendant, is unnamed. She represents virtue and chastity.
  • Comus (villan): The antagonist, a sorcerer and the son of Bacchus (god of wine) and Circe (a sorceress in Greek mythology). He embodies temptation and indulgence.
  • Attendant Spirit: A benevolent spirit who guides and protects the Lady.
  • Sabrina: Goddes of river Sabrina.
  • Circe:- A kind witch
  • John Milton's Comus, also known as A Maske Presented at Ludlow Castle, is an allegory that explores the themes of good versus evil, temperance versus indulgence, and the importance of chastity. Here's a quick summary of the story: Two brothers travel with their younger sister (simply called "the Lady") through a dark forest. The Lady becomes separated from her brothers and encounters Comus, a deceptive sorcerer disguised as a helpful villager. Comus tempts the Lady with promises of pleasure and a magical potion, but she resists his advances thanks to her strong moral compass. He imprisons her in his enchanting palace, but the Lady remains resolute in her virtue. With the help of a magical Attendant Spirit, the brothers find their sister and free her from Comus's spell. A nymph named Sabrina helps break the spell completely, and the reunited siblings return home. Comus represents the temptations of sensuality and vice, while the Lady embodies virtue and self-control. The play celebrates the importance of resisting these temptations and staying true to one's morals.
