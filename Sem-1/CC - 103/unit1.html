  • Alankara (figures of speech)
  • Indian poetics emphasizes the importance of the interplay between shabda (word) and artha (meaning). Words are not mere vessels for meaning; their sound and form also contribute to the overall experience. The concept of dhvani (suggestion) further elaborates on this. A poem's true meaning often lies not just in the literal words but in what they suggest or evoke in the reader's mind.
  • Rasa(Aesthetic Experience)
  • Nine(9) Primary Rasas are:-
  • Sringar Rasa (Love)
  • Hasya Rasa(Laughter)
  • Karuna Rasa(Pathos)
  • Raudra Rasa(Anger)
  • Vira Rasa(Heroism)
  • Bhayanakara Rasa(Fear)
  • Vibhatsa Rasa(Disgust)
  • Adbhuta Rasa(wonder)
  • Shanta Rasa(Peace)
  • Rasa(emotions presented love, anger, compassion, heroism, etc. ):- The essence of Indian poetics, Rasa refers to the aesthetic experience or emotional response evoked in the reader by the poem. It's not simply about conveying emotions but creating a space where the reader can temporarily identify with the emotions presented.Different schools of thought propose various Rasas, with the most common being love, anger, compassion, heroism, etc.
  • Dhwani:-
  • Beyond the literal meaning of words.
  • Suggested meaning can be more powerful and nuanced than the literal meaning.
  • Adds depth and complexity to poetry
  • Dhwani:- As mentioned earlier, Dhwani refers to the suggestive or implied meaning in a poem. It's the underlying layer that goes beyond the literal and creates the true impact of the Rasa.
  • Alankar
  • Alankara refers to figures of speech or ornamentation used in poetry to enhance the beauty,clarity, and impact of the poem.Used to create vivid imagery, evoke emotions,emphasize ideas and play with language in creative ways.
  • Alankar(metaphors, similes, personification, hyperbole, etc.):-
  • Metaphors(direct comparison of two things that aren't alike, but share some important characteristic.)
  • Similes(comparison of similarities.)
  • Personification(Personification is a figure of speech that breathes life into things that aren't alive! It does this by giving human characteristics to (objects, ideas, or animals.))
  • Hyperbole(extreme exaggeration,strong emotions). eg."I'm so hungry I could eat a horse."
  • Vakrokti(Twisting of Words):-
  • Refers to the clever and unexpected use of words
  • to create surprise,humor and new insights
  • use to challenge conventions,and reveal hidden meanings by using:-
  • Irony
  • Paradox
  • Ambiguity
  • Vakrokti("saying things in a twisted way."):- This concept emphasizes the importance of "saying things in a twisted way." It's not about being obscure but using language creatively and with a twist to convey a deeper meaning or surprise the reader.
