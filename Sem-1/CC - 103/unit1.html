Indian Poetics: Function and Features of Poetry, Purpose of Poetry, The Word and Meaning, Rasa, Dhwani, Alankar, Vakrokti.

Function and Features of Poetry, Purpose of Poetry:- Function: Unlike some Western theories that focus on instruction or catharsis, Indian poetics view poetry primarily as a means of aesthetic pleasure (Rasa) and spiritual upliftment.
Features: Poetry achieves this through various features, including:-
Evocative Language: Words are carefully chosen for their sound, imagery, and ability to suggest emotions and ideas beyond the literal meaning.
Figurative Language: Similes, metaphors, and other figures of speech create vivid imagery and add depth to the poem.
Rhythmic Patterns: Meter and rhyme schemes create a musicality that enhances the poem's impact.
Structure: The overall structure of the poem, with its stanzas and organization, contributes to the flow and development of ideas.

The Word and Meaning:- Indian poetics emphasizes the importance of the interplay between shabda (word) and artha (meaning). Words are not mere vessels for meaning; their sound and form also contribute to the overall experience. The concept of dhvani (suggestion) further elaborates on this. A poem's true meaning often lies not just in the literal words but in what they suggest or evoke in the reader's mind.

Core Concepts:-
Rasa:- The essence of Indian poetics, Rasa refers to the aesthetic experience or emotional response evoked in the reader by the poem. It's not simply about conveying emotions but creating a space where the reader can temporarily identify with the emotions presented.Different schools of thought propose various Rasas, with the most common being love, anger, compassion, heroism, etc.
Dhwani:- As mentioned earlier, Dhwani refers to the suggestive or implied meaning in a poem. It's the underlying layer that goes beyond the literal and creates the true impact of the Rasa.
Alankar:- Alankara refers to figures of speech or ornamentation used in poetry. These include metaphors, similes, personification, hyperbole, etc. While not the sole focus, Alankara adds beauty, depth, and complexity to the poem.
Vakrokti:- This concept emphasizes the importance of "saying things in a twisted way." It's not about being obscure but using language creatively and with a twist to convey a deeper meaning or surprise the reader.
